Terms and Conditions

Last updated: February 2024

1. Acceptance of Terms

By accessing and using WALANTU Trade Services ("the Platform"), you accept and agree to be bound by these Terms and Conditions. If you do not agree to these terms, please do not use our services.

2. Services Description

WALANTU Trade Services connects customers with verified tradespeople for various maintenance and repair services in Accra, Ghana. We act as an intermediary platform and are not direct service providers.

3. Booking Process

  • All bookings require a GH₵ 50 non-refundable assessment fee
  • Customers must provide accurate GhanaPostGPS addresses
  • National ID verification is required for all bookings
  • Bookings are confirmed only after payment of assessment fee

4. Payments and Fees

  • Assessment fee: GH₵ 50 (non-refundable)
  • Daily rates: GH₵ 150-300 depending on service category
  • Distance fee: GH₵ 0.50 per km beyond 10km radius
  • Full payment is required upon job completion and customer satisfaction

5. Work Guarantee

All jobs completed through WALANTU include a standard 3-month workmanship guarantee. Extended insurance options (3, 6, or 12 months) are available at additional cost.

6. Cancellation Policy

  • Cancellations within 24 hours of booking: Full assessment fee refund
  • Cancellations after professional dispatch: Assessment fee forfeited
  • Same-day cancellations may incur additional charges

7. Liability and Insurance

WALANTU maintains general liability insurance. Extended insurance options provide additional coverage for parts and labor beyond the standard 3-month period.

8. Privacy and Data Protection

We collect and process personal data in accordance with Ghana's Data Protection Act. Your National ID and contact information are stored securely and used only for service delivery and verification purposes.

9. Dispute Resolution

Any disputes arising from services provided shall be resolved through:

  1. Direct negotiation with the tradesperson
  2. Mediation through WALANTU customer service
  3. Legal proceedings in Accra courts if unresolved
